Diagnosis

Also known as X-Ray Department and/or Radiology Department.The radiology (x-ray) department at Clinical provides a diagnostic service for the Trust and the wider community. Operating under the Ministry of Health of Romania guidelines, the radiology department performs over 10,000 examinations a year, across all specialities. The Diagnosis department at Clinical is proud to have been QSI accredited since 2014. QSI is a patient-focussed assessment and accreditation programme that is designed to help diagnostic imaging services ensure that their patients consistently receive high-quality services, delivered by competent staff working in safe environments. To maintain this accreditation, we annually provide evidence to ensure we are meeting this excellent standard, to assure our patients and service users that we provide a high-quality service.

Surgery

Every year, thousands of patients choose Clinical for needed cardiac surgeries — from simple, minimally invasive procedures to complex and exacting operations. Clinical surgeons perform high volumes of complex cardiac operations. Our caring staff take the time to explain the details and make sure all questions are answered. Surgery at Clinical offers several advantages, including efficient scheduling for complex care, a broad spectrum of services, use of the latest technology, and access to clinical trials and new treatments. The Mission of the Department of Surgery is the pursuit of excellence in comprehensive, patient-centered surgical care, teaching, research and evaluation of technology.

Emergency

Emergency department: The department of a hospital responsible for the provision of medical and surgical care to patients arriving at the hospital in need of immediate care. Emergency department personnel may also respond to certain situations within the hospital such cardiac arrests. EDs have highly trained doctors and other health professionals on site to deal with emergencies. They assess, treat, stabilise and start the health management of people who have come to the ED with a serious illness or injury. Some emergency departments specialise. For example, there are emergency department attached to children’s hospitals, some women’s hospitals . Most EDs, however, accept all emergencies. When you go to an ED, the first step is usually for an administrative staff member to get your name, address and Medicare number. This is important to make sure that you receive the treatment you need, and to avoid confusing your healthcare management with anybody else’s. This step is bypassed in an emergency.
